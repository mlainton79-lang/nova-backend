Dear Sir or Madam,

I write to make a formal complaint against Western Circle Ltd, trading as Cashfloat, an FCA-authorised consumer credit firm, regarding their irresponsible lending practices and their failure to treat me as a vulnerable customer in accordance with FCA rules, specifically the Consumer Duty (PS22/9) and CONC 5 (Responsible Lending).

BACKGROUND

Western Circle Ltd advanced high-cost short-term credit to me at a time when I was a vulnerable customer. They subsequently obtained a County Court Judgment (CCJ) against me for approximately £700 (reference K9QZ4X9N). I have raised my concerns directly with Western Circle through their internal complaints process on multiple occasions. They have acknowledged my vulnerability claims in correspondence but have maintained that their affordability checks at the time of lending were sufficient and that the CCJ was lawfully obtained. They have refused to remove the CCJ and have directed me to apply to the court to have it set aside, rather than accepting any responsibility for their lending decision.

I have now exhausted Western Circle's internal complaints process without satisfactory resolution and am therefore escalating this matter to the FCA.

GROUNDS FOR COMPLAINT

1. FAILURE TO CONDUCT ADEQUATE AFFORDABILITY ASSESSMENTS (CONC 5)

Under CONC 5.2, lenders must undertake a reasonable assessment of creditworthiness before advancing credit. At the time Western Circle lent to me, I was experiencing financial difficulty related to problem gambling — a recognised vulnerability under FCA guidance. A proper affordability assessment, conducted with due diligence, should have identified that I was not in a position to sustainably repay the credit advanced. Western Circle's own correspondence acknowledges my vulnerability but states their checks were "sufficient." I dispute this. An affordability assessment that fails to identify an active gambling problem and its effect on disposable income cannot be considered adequate under CONC 5.

2. FAILURE TO TREAT ME AS A VULNERABLE CUSTOMER (FCA CONSUMER DUTY / FG21/1)

The FCA's Guidance for firms on the fair treatment of vulnerable customers (FG21/1) and the Consumer Duty (effective July 2023) require firms to take particular care with vulnerable customers and ensure they achieve good outcomes. Western Circle acknowledged in their correspondence that I raised vulnerability as a factor. However, their response was to maintain the debt and pursue legal recovery rather than to consider appropriate forbearance, a reduced settlement, or withdrawal of the CCJ. This is not consistent with the good outcomes required by the Consumer Duty.

3. FAILURE TO EXERCISE APPROPRIATE FORBEARANCE

Under CONC 7.3, firms must treat customers in financial difficulty with forbearance and due consideration. Rather than offering genuine forbearance — such as freezing interest, accepting a reduced settlement, or withdrawing the CCJ — Western Circle pursued a County Court Judgment and has since refused to remove it despite my repeated requests and evidence of vulnerability.

4. THE CCJ IS THE DIRECT RESULT OF IRRESPONSIBLE LENDING

Had Western Circle conducted proper affordability checks and applied appropriate vulnerability provisions, the credit should not have been advanced in the first place. The CCJ is therefore a direct consequence of their regulatory failures. The appropriate remedy is removal of the CCJ and cancellation of the associated debt.

WHAT I AM SEEKING

I am requesting that the FCA investigate Western Circle Ltd's conduct in this matter and that, as a result of this investigation:

1. The CCJ (reference K9QZ4X9N) be removed from the public register and from my credit file.
2. The debt of approximately £700 be written off in full.
3. Western Circle be required to review their affordability and vulnerability assessment processes.
4. Compensation be considered for the distress and financial harm caused by this CCJ remaining on my credit file.

If the FCA is unable to act directly on my individual case, I will simultaneously be referring this matter to the Financial Ombudsman Service for individual redress.

SUPPORTING EVIDENCE

I hold copies of all correspondence with Western Circle, including their responses to my vulnerability claims and their refusal to remove the CCJ. I am happy to provide these upon request.

Yours faithfully,

Matthew Lainton
